# Nursing Module — Altruistic Framework

**Date:** 2026-05-13  
**Scope:** Human-centered principles for the Telangana Staff Nurse practice module.  
**Author:** Kimi Code CLI  

## 1. Definition

An **altruistic framework** means every design and engineering decision is evaluated against one question:

> *"Does this help the learner pass the exam with dignity, confidence, and minimal suffering?"*

If a feature is technically clever but increases anxiety, complexity, or cost without clear benefit, it is rejected.

## 2. The Learner's Context

The primary learner is the user's mother, but the framework should generalise to any Telangana nursing recruitment candidate:

- **Time-poor:** Studies between household and work responsibilities.
- **Language-negotiator:** May be more comfortable in Telugu but must master English exam terms.
- **Outcome-focused:** Needs a government job for financial stability.
- **Anxiety-prone:** High-stakes exam; previous failures may be present.
- **Device-limited:** Likely smartphone-first, possibly intermittent data.

## 3. Altruistic Principles

### 3.1 Respect the learner's time
- Every screen should have a clear purpose.
- No unnecessary animations, onboarding, or upsells.
- Short sessions (5–15 minutes) must feel valuable.

### 3.2 Respect the learner's intelligence
- No condescending language.
- Explanations should teach, not just state answers.
- Adult-appropriate visual design.

### 3.3 Respect the learner's emotional state
- Calm colour palette.
- Progress is framed positively.
- Wrong answers are opportunities, not failures.

### 3.4 Respect the learner's constraints
- Works on low-end smartphones.
- Offline PDF export.
- Minimal data usage.
- No account creation friction.

### 3.5 Respect the learner's trust
- Source every medical fact.
- Easy way to report errors.
- Clear disclaimer.
- No dark patterns (e.g., fake scarcity, forced notifications).

## 4. Altruistic Decision Filter

Before adding any feature, ask:

1. **Need:** Does the learner actually need this, or do we just think it is cool?
2. **Access:** Can she use it given her device, language, and connectivity?
3. **Burden:** Does it add cognitive load, time, or anxiety?
4. **Truth:** Is the information accurate and verifiable?
5. **Dignity:** Does it treat her as a capable adult?
6. **Sustainability:** Can we maintain this without burning out?

A feature must pass at least 5 of 6 to be included in v1.

## 5. Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Altruistic Replacement |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Gamification with childish badges | Meaningful progress bars and mastery indicators |
| Leaderboards comparing her to strangers | Personal streak and accuracy trends |
| Unlimited notifications | Optional daily reminder, default off |
| Locked content requiring payment | All content free, open source |
| English-only with no support | English questions + Telugu glossary/UI |
| Guessing the syllabus | Document sources and admit uncertainty |

## 6. Success Metrics (Altruistic)

- She uses it at least 4 times per week.
- Her mock-test scores improve over 4 weeks.
- She reports feeling "more confident" (qualitative).
- Reported question rate is low (< 2%).
- No wrong medical answers persist > 7 days.

## 7. Long-Term Altruistic Vision

A free, verified, multilingual practice platform for healthcare worker recruitment exams across India. Built by learners, for learners. No paywalls. No surveillance. No condescension.
